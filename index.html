Roots of a Quadratic Equation

The roots of a quadratic equation are the values of the variable, x, that satisfy the equation: Ax² + Bx + C = 0 .
They are also referred to as the "solutions" or "zeros" of the quadratic equation.

By applying the quadratic formula, we can take the coefficients and find not only the roots, but their product and sum as well.
